Diol Index for upwelling and/or nutrient conditions

Long chain 1,14-diols are often found in high-nutrient environments like upwelling areas (see fig. 1). (1)

In the Arabian Sea, it was observed that long chain 1,14-diol concentrations, produced by Proboscia diatoms, only increased during upwelling of nutrient rich waters in the Southwest Monsoon, whereas C30 1,15-diol concentrations increased both during upwelling and in the Northeast Monsoon period when deep mixing of the water column brought the nutrients back to the surface (3). Based on those observations, the Diol Index was introduced, where long chain 1,14-diol concentrations were expressed relative to the concentrations of 1,14-diols and the C30 1,15-diol. This index was tested on a sediment record spanning the last 90 kyr (fig 2)

1,14-diols are also often abundant in Arctic and Antarctic waters. However, in these cold environments, C30 1,15-diol concentrations are generally low or absent. Therefore, it was suggested to modify the Diol Index for those conditions by expressing long chain 1,14-diol concentrations relative to the combined 1,13- and 1,14-diol concentrations (4). That study on the shelf waters of the western Antarctic Peninsula over the last 8500 yr, demonstrated that the climatic processes responsible for changes in mean summer temperature, open marine influence and atmospheric cyclonic activity recorded at Siple Dome, also controlled the productivity of Proboscia diatoms on the western Antarctic Peninsula region.
